# DETAILED CHAPTER OUTLINES: CAVE MOUTHS (Chapters 1-15)

## Chapter 1: Low Tide Discovery

**Target Word Count:** 1,700 words
**POV:** Third person, alternating Brennan/Morrison
**Setting:** Depoe Bay, Oregon - sea cave crime scene, coastal highway
**Time:** Morning, July 1978, overcast with fog

**Purpose:** Establish investigators, crime scene, and immediate tension between FBI methodology and local resistance to murder theory.

**Beats:**
- Brennan drives Highway 101 into Depoe Bay, first impressions of coastal town during tourist season, arrives at crime scene staging area where Sheriff Garland is dismissive and territorial about FBI involvement
- Morrison leads her down to cave entrance accessible at low tide, both navigate rocky terrain, discover victim Jennifer Kowalski positioned with hands reaching toward carved symbols on basalt wall
- Crime scene examination with Brennan photographing everything methodically while Morrison questions positioning, she identifies ritualistic staging while he suggests tidal movement could explain it, Sheriff insists accidental drowning
- Tide coming in forces evacuation, tension as Brennan wants more time but ocean won't wait, Morrison explains tide danger while respecting her need for thoroughness
- Brief post-scene conversation where Brennan requests previous coastal death files, Morrison surprised by implication of pattern, chapter ends with her certainty this is serial murder

**Dialogue Topics:** Jurisdictional protocols, victim identification, accidental drowning versus homicide, tide table timing, Garland's resistance to FBI presence, Morrison's neutral professionalism masking skepticism, Brennan's pattern recognition instincts.

**Emotional Arc:** Brennan's controlled intensity meeting Morrison's weathered caution. Initial professional distance, mutual assessment, no warmth yet but competent respect beginning to form.

**Key Details:** 1978 Ford vehicles, film cameras, rotary phone coordination, weather-beaten motel victim worked at, carved symbols photographed, blunt force trauma visible, fog and salt air atmosphere.

**Key Details:** Dusty file boxes, handwritten logs, typed incident reports, carbon copies, basement with inadequate lighting, manual cross-referencing without computers, coffee breaks during long research day.
